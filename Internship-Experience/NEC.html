My Project Internship at NEC Technologies

During my tenure at NEC Technologies, I had the privilege of working on a research project titled "Integrity Protection for CAN Messages in Connected Car Systems." This opportunity came after leveraging my subject knowledge and curiosity to explore new domains, which proved instrumental in acing the interview.

Assigned as the sole contributor to this project, I worked autonomously across the realms of 5G Technology, CAN-Bus communication, automotive security, and embedded system security. This culminated in a comprehensive paper presenting the application of cryptographic techniques to ensure the integrity of CAN messages in connected cars.

The dedication and innovation I brought to this project were commended by the Assistant Vice President at NEC, reinforcing my belief in the power of individual initiative. In recognition of my achievements, I was awarded a stipend of INR 15,000 upon successful completion.

I also chose to present the same project as my final year college endeavor — earning a distinction as the only individual in my college to undertake and complete this project independently, bringing significant recognition to the institution.

This experience honed my technical skills while instilling a sense of self-reliance and commitment to excellence. It stands as a testament to my capacity to excel in challenging, research-intensive environments and my dedication to driving innovation in the field of automotive security.
