My Journey as a student at UTD

I am currently pursuing higher education at the University of Texas at Dallas (UTD), a renowned institution known for its excellence in academics and cutting-edge programs. Here, I undertook the prestigious Masters in Business Analytics program, which stands as the largest program of its kind in the United States, encompassing a comprehensive 36-credit curriculum.

During my time at UTD, I delved into a diverse range of courses that not only broadened my understanding but also equipped me with practical skills crucial for the field of Business Analytics. Some of the standout courses I engaged in include:

  • Business Analytics with R
  • Database Foundations for Business Analytics
  • Advanced Statistics for Data Science
  • Professional Development
  • Big Data
  • Cloud Computing Fundamentals
  • Applied Machine Learning
  • Prescriptive Analytics
  • Econometrics and Time Series Analysis
  • Data Visualization
  • Marketing Web Analytics
  • Predictive Analytics using SAS
  • Natural Language Processing

UTD not only provided a rigorous academic environment but also served as a platform to apply the knowledge I gained into real-world applications, solidifying my grasp on the subject matter.

In addition to my studies, I also took on the role of a Graduate Teaching Assistant, contributing to the undergraduate course 'Information Technology for Business'. This invaluable experience not only honed my teaching abilities but also sharpened my own skills.

Beyond academics, I immersed myself in various extracurricular activities. Holding the position of Associate Vice President at the Society of Operations Research and Management Sciences (INFORMS) Club at UTD, I spearheaded a dedicated events team responsible for organizing presentations, event planning, execution, and overseeing logistical aspects. This role also involved active participation in club meetings, as well as managing room bookings.

Furthermore, I had the privilege of being a part of the esteemed Business Analytics Leadership Council. As a member of the mentoring team, I extended guidance to both current and incoming MSBA students, offering insights and assistance through the mentorship program, drawing from my own experience

Overall, my journey at UTD has been nothing short of remarkable. I am set to graduate in Spring 2024 (May 2024), and I am eager to channel the wealth of knowledge and skills I've acquired into meaningful contributions in the field of Business Analytics.
